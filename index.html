Origin

This is a story about how remaining silent can cause the world to become a meaner place.

To start, let's try understanding the concept behind this story called The Spiral of Silence.

The inhabitants in this world that we will be exploring have two different opinions, A and B. Circles represent opinion A and the triangles represent opinion B.

Fitting In

Notice that there are many triangles, which means that opinion A is the majority opinion. Since the circles have different opinions from the majority, they are being excluded from the main group of shapes. In other words, the circles are being isolated.

Fitting In

We all love big groups, don’t we? However, to be included in that group, we will have to change our opinions to match the majority’s. Try moving the lonely circles to the other side!

But...wait! What if we don’t actually change our opinions to fit in? What if we pretend to do so instead, even though we do not agree with them?

Time's Ticking

As time passes, we are exposed to the majority opinion and less of the minority opinion. As such, we start to actually conform to the majority opinion. Toggle the "Present" button to see how opinions can change over time.

So far, we’ve seen that the circles will always try to fit in with the triangles, because the triangles are the majority. But this is what is called a perceived majority. In other words, this opinion is only thought to be the majority.

Not What Meets The Eye

Sometimes, the perceived majority is not the actual majority. Toggle the "Perceived" button and observe what happens. The circles are the majority opinion. This is called the actual majority. However, the circles still perceive the triangles as the majority opinion and will try to fit in with them.

What is the Spiral of Silence?
"More frightened of isolation than of committing an error, they joined the masses even though they did not agree with them."

Elisabeth Noelle-Neumann (1916-2010)

However, don’t underestimate the power of what an individual can do! Even if someone else has a different opinion, you can voice out your opinion to try to convince them. Try moving the circle closer to the triangle and make it speak up!
