BIG  IDEAS

UIT2207.

Avocading big ideas, one model at a time.

Origin

This is a story about how remaining silent can cause the world to become a meaner place.

To start, let's try understanding the concept behind this story called The Spiral of Silence.

Fitting In

We all love big groups, don’t we? However, to be included in that group, we will have to change our opinions to match the majority’s. Try moving the lonely white circles to the other side! What happens to them?
But...wait! What if we don’t actually change our opinions to fit in? What if we pretend to do so instead? Try toggling the time button. Do the shape and color change?
Over time, integrating with the majority can change our opinions, even if we began with different opinions.



Not What Meets The Eye

So far, we’ve seen that the white circles will always try to fit in with the triangles, because the triangles are the majority. But this is what is called a perceived majority. In other words, this opinion is thought to be the majority, but it actually isn’t. In the following model, toggling the button will show us that the white circles are the majority opinion. This is called the actual majority. However, toggling the button again will show you that the white circles still think the triangles are the majority opinion - the perceived majority - and so they will still try to fit in with the triangles.
